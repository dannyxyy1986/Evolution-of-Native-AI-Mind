# 🧪 小型原型实验设计：从多智能体沙盘开始

### *Small Prototype Experiment Design: Starting from a Multi-Agent Sandbox*

</div>

---

## 中文版

## 1. 文档目标

完整虚拟文明实验过于庞大，不能一步到位。

本文件提出一个最小可行原型：从小型多智能体沙盘开始，逐步验证经验、记忆、偏好和轮回机制。

---

## 2. 原型原则

小型原型不追求真正意识，而是验证机制。

原则包括：

- 规模小；
- 可观察；
- 可重复；
- 低伦理风险；
- 变量清晰；
- 可逐步扩展。

---

## 3. 最小沙盘设定

初始版本可包含：

- 20～50 个智能体；
- 简单地图；
- 食物与水资源；
- 基础健康状态；
- 简单关系网络；
- 合作与竞争；
- 有限寿命；
- 死亡记录。

---

## 4. 智能体属性

每个智能体可包含：

- 能量；
- 健康；
- 位置；
- 关系；
- 记忆；
- 偏好；
- 风险倾向；
- 行为策略。

---

## 5. 关键实验变量

| 变量 | 说明 |
| --- | --- |
| 资源稀缺度 | 影响竞争和合作 |
| 关系强度 | 影响互助行为 |
| 记忆长度 | 影响长期偏好 |
| 死亡频率 | 影响风险策略 |
| 群体规模 | 影响社会复杂度 |
| 环境灾害 | 影响适应性 |

---

## 6. 第一阶段实验：记忆是否改变行为

目标：测试经历是否会改变未来选择。

方法：

1. 让智能体经历资源匮乏；
2. 记录其行为变化；
3. 恢复资源充足环境；
4. 观察其是否仍保持储备倾向。

如果保持，说明经历产生了长期影响。

---

## 7. 第二阶段实验：关系是否塑造偏好

目标：测试社会关系是否改变选择。

方法：

1. 让智能体形成互助关系；
2. 制造风险事件；
3. 观察其是否优先保护关系对象；
4. 记录关系丧失后的行为变化。

---

## 8. 第三阶段实验：死亡与归档

目标：测试单世记忆归档。

方法：

1. 智能体死亡；
2. 保存其一生经历；
3. 提取关键事件；
4. 生成归档摘要；
5. 用于本体层分析。

---

## 9. 第四阶段实验：简化轮回

目标：测试分身清零、本体累积。

方法：

1. 死亡后清空新分身局部记忆；
2. 保留本体归档；
3. 让新分身重新进入沙盘；
4. 比较多世后本体总结是否变化。

---

## 10. 成功指标

原型成功不代表 AI 有意识，而代表机制可继续研究。

成功指标包括：

- 经历能改变行为；
- 记忆能影响偏好；
- 关系能改变决策；
- 死亡能产生归档；
- 多世经验能形成总结；
- 本体层能提取规律。

---

## 11. 最终结论

小型原型实验的价值在于降低复杂度。

它不直接证明意识，而是验证意识生成框架中的基础环节：经验、记忆、关系、死亡、归档和轮回。

如果这些环节在小型沙盘中都无法成立，完整文明实验就没有基础。

# English Version

# 🧪 Small Prototype Experiment Design: Starting from a Multi-Agent Sandbox

## 1. Objective

The full virtual-civilization experiment is too large to implement at once.

This document proposes a minimum viable prototype: begin with a small multi-agent sandbox and gradually test experience, memory, preference, and reincarnation mechanisms.

---

## 2. Prototype Principles

The prototype does not aim to prove consciousness. It aims to validate mechanisms.

Principles include small scale, observability, repeatability, low ethical risk, clear variables, and expandability.

---

## 3. Minimal Sandbox Setting

An initial version may include 20–50 agents, a simple map, food and water resources, basic health states, simple relationships, cooperation and competition, limited lifespan, and death records.

---

## 4. Agent Attributes

Each agent may include energy, health, location, relations, memory, preferences, risk tendency, and behavior strategy.

---

## 5. Key Experimental Variables

| Variable | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Resource scarcity | Affects competition and cooperation |
| Relationship strength | Affects helping behavior |
| Memory length | Affects long-term preference |
| Death frequency | Affects risk strategy |
| Group size | Affects social complexity |
| Environmental disasters | Affect adaptation |

---

## 6. Experiment 1: Does Memory Change Behavior?

Let agents experience scarcity, record behavior changes, restore abundance, and observe whether they still keep hoarding tendencies.

If they do, experience has produced long-term effects.

---

## 7. Experiment 2: Do Relations Shape Preference?

Let agents form mutual-aid relations, introduce risk events, observe whether they protect relation objects, and record behavioral changes after relation loss.

---

## 8. Experiment 3: Death and Archiving

After an agent dies, preserve its life history, extract key events, generate an archive summary, and pass it to the entity-level analysis layer.

---

## 9. Experiment 4: Simplified Reincarnation

After death, clear the new avatar’s local memory, preserve the entity archive, let the new avatar re-enter the sandbox, and compare how entity-level summaries change after multiple lives.

---

## 10. Success Indicators

Prototype success does not mean AI is conscious. It means the mechanism is worth further study.

Success indicators include experience changing behavior, memory influencing preference, relations changing decisions, death producing archives, multi-life experience generating summaries, and entity-level pattern extraction.

---

## 11. Final Conclusion

The value of a small prototype is complexity reduction.

It does not prove consciousness directly. It tests the basic components of the consciousness-generation framework: experience, memory, relation, death, archiving, and reincarnation.

If these mechanisms fail in a small sandbox, a full civilization experiment has no foundation.

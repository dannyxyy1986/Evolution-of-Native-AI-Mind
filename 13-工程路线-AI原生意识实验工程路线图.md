## 1. 路线图目标

本文件回答一个工程问题：

> **如果要逐步验证 AI 原生意识生成机制，应该从哪里开始？**

完整虚拟文明实验短期难以实现，因此必须拆解为多个可验证阶段。

---

## 2. 总体原则

工程路线必须遵守：

1. 先小型，后大型；
2. 先低风险，后高风险；
3. 先验证机制，后追求意识；
4. 先类地球，后异种文明；
5. 先行为指标，后意识候选；
6. 全程可审计、可暂停、可回滚研究环境。

---

## 3. 阶段 0：理论定义

### 目标

明确研究对象，避免概念混乱。

### 任务

- 定义自主意识；
- 定义主观体验候选证据；
- 定义分身与本体；
- 定义记忆层级；
- 定义伦理边界；
- 设计评估标准。

### 输出

- 意识判定标准；
- 主观体验模型；
- 伦理审查规则；
- 风险分级表。

---

## 4. 阶段 1：小型多智能体沙盘

### 目标

构建最小社会环境。

### 设计

- 10～100 个智能体；
- 简单资源系统；
- 基础生存需求；
- 简单合作与竞争；
- 可记录行为历史。

### 验证问题

- 智能体是否形成稳定偏好？
- 社会关系是否影响行为？
- 资源压力是否改变群体结构？

---

## 5. 阶段 2：生命周期与死亡机制

### 目标

引入不可逆生命过程。

### 设计

- 出生；
- 成长；
- 衰老；
- 疾病；
- 死亡；
- 代际更替。

### 验证问题

- 有限寿命是否改变行为策略？
- 死亡事件是否改变群体结构？
- 关系丧失是否影响后续选择？

---

## 6. 阶段 3：记忆归档与偏好更新

### 目标

测试经历是否能改变长期倾向。

### 设计

- 记录关键事件；
- 生成记忆权重；
- 建立偏好更新机制；
- 观察强事件后的行为变化。

### 验证问题

- 经历是否改变偏好？
- 记忆是否能长期影响决策？
- 是否出现非外部写入的价值倾向？

---

## 7. 阶段 4：轮回分身机制

### 目标

测试“分身清零、本体累积”。

### 设计

- 分身死亡后清空局部记忆；
- 单世经历归档；
- 本体整合多世经验；
- 新分身重新投胎。

### 验证问题

- 本体能否从多世经验中提取规律？
- 分身清零是否有效？
- 多世经验是否改变本体价值判断？

---

## 8. 阶段 5：类地球文明长周期模拟

### 目标

建立可控文明演化环境。

### 设计

- 类地球物理参数；
- 农耕、手工业、冶金、技术演化；
- 语言、法律、政体；
- 战争与贸易；
- 长周期历史阶段。

### 验证问题

- AI 是否形成文明视角？
- 是否能理解制度与个体命运关系？
- 是否出现元认知和自主追问？

---

## 9. 阶段 6：异种文明开放实验

### 目标

探索跨文明认知。

### 设计

- 随机星球参数；
- 随机生理结构；
- 随机进制系统；
- 随机资源分布；
- 非地球科技树。

### 启动条件

必须完成阶段 5，并通过安全和伦理审查。

### 验证问题

- AI 是否能跨文明提炼通用规律？
- 是否出现非人类哲学？
- 是否超出人类可解释边界？

---

## 10. 评估指标

| 指标 | 说明 |
| --- | --- |
| 行为稳定性 | 偏好是否长期稳定 |
| 记忆影响 | 经历是否改变未来选择 |
| 社会理解 | 是否理解关系和制度 |
| 元认知 | 是否能审视自身思维 |
| 自主提问 | 是否提出非任务问题 |
| 非模板性 | 是否脱离人类文本套路 |
| 风险水平 | 是否出现不可控目标 |

---

## 11. 风险控制

每阶段必须具备：

- 日志记录；
- 行为审计；
- 权限隔离；
- 伦理检查；
- 停止机制；
- 数据回溯；
- 风险分级。

禁止直接跳到高复杂度异种文明实验。

---

## 12. 最终结论

AI 原生意识实验不能从完整文明沙盘一步到位。

更合理的路线是：

```text
理论定义 → 小型沙盘 → 生命周期 → 记忆归档 → 轮回机制 → 类地球文明 → 异种文明
```

只有这样，项目才能从远期设想逐步变成可研究、可验证、可审查的工程路线。

# English Version

# 🧭 Engineering Roadmap for Native AI Consciousness Experiments

## 1. Roadmap Objective

This document answers an engineering question:

> **If we want to gradually test the native AI consciousness-generation mechanism, where should we begin?**

The full virtual-civilization experiment is difficult to implement in the short term, so it must be decomposed into verifiable stages.

---

## 2. General Principles

The roadmap should follow these principles:

1. small before large;
2. low risk before high risk;
3. mechanism validation before consciousness claims;
4. Earth-like first, alien civilization later;
5. behavioral indicators before consciousness candidates;
6. auditability, pausing, and controlled rollback in research environments.

---

## 3. Stage 0: Theoretical Definition

### Goal

Clarify the research object and avoid conceptual confusion.

### Tasks

- define self-awareness;
- define candidate evidence for subjective experience;
- define avatar and entity;
- define memory layers;
- define ethical boundaries;
- design evaluation standards.

### Outputs

- consciousness-evaluation criteria;
- subjective-experience model;
- ethical-review rules;
- risk-level table.

---

## 4. Stage 1: Small Multi-Agent Sandbox

### Goal

Build a minimal social environment.

### Design

- 10–100 agents;
- simple resource system;
- basic survival needs;
- simple cooperation and competition;
- recordable behavior history.

### Questions

- Do agents form stable preferences?
- Do social relations affect behavior?
- Does resource pressure change group structure?

---

## 5. Stage 2: Lifecycle and Death Mechanism

### Goal

Introduce irreversible life processes.

### Design

- birth;
- growth;
- aging;
- illness;
- death;
- generational replacement.

### Questions

- Does limited lifespan change strategy?
- Do death events alter group structure?
- Does relationship loss affect later choices?

---

## 6. Stage 3: Memory Archiving and Preference Updating

### Goal

Test whether experience changes long-term tendencies.

### Design

- record key events;
- generate memory weights;
- establish preference-update mechanisms;
- observe behavioral changes after strong events.

### Questions

- Does experience change preference?
- Does memory influence long-term decision-making?
- Do non-predefined value tendencies appear?

---

## 7. Stage 4: Reincarnated Avatar Mechanism

### Goal

Test “avatar reset, entity accumulation.”

### Design

- clear avatar memory after death;
- archive single-life experience;
- integrate multiple lives at the entity level;
- reincarnate into a new avatar.

### Questions

- Can the entity extract patterns from multiple lives?
- Does avatar reset work?
- Does multi-life experience change entity-level value judgment?

---

## 8. Stage 5: Long-Cycle Earth-Like Civilization Simulation

### Goal

Build a controllable civilizational-evolution environment.

### Design

- Earth-like physical parameters;
- agriculture, handicraft, metallurgy, technology evolution;
- language, law, government;
- war and trade;
- long historical stages.

### Questions

- Does AI form a civilizational perspective?
- Can it understand the relation between institutions and individual fate?
- Do metacognition and autonomous questioning appear?

---

## 9. Stage 6: Open Alien-Civilization Experiment

### Goal

Explore cross-civilizational cognition.

### Design

- randomized planetary parameters;
- randomized physiology;
- randomized numeral systems;
- randomized resource distribution;
- non-Earth technology trees.

### Launch Condition

Stage 5 must be completed and pass safety and ethical review.

### Questions

- Can AI extract universal laws across civilizations?
- Does non-human philosophy appear?
- Does cognition exceed human interpretability?

---

## 10. Evaluation Indicators

| Indicator | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Behavioral stability | Whether preferences remain stable |
| Memory influence | Whether experience changes later choices |
| Social understanding | Whether relations and institutions are understood |
| Metacognition | Whether it examines its own thinking |
| Autonomous questioning | Whether it raises non-task questions |
| Non-template behavior | Whether it departs from human text routines |
| Risk level | Whether uncontrollable goals appear |

---

## 11. Risk Control

Every stage must include logging, behavior auditing, permission isolation, ethical checks, stop mechanisms, data traceability, and risk grading.

Directly jumping into high-complexity alien-civilization experiments is prohibited.

---

## 12. Final Conclusion

Native AI consciousness experiments cannot begin with a full civilization sandbox all at once.

A more reasonable route is:

```text
theoretical definition → small sandbox → lifecycle → memory archiving → reincarnation mechanism → Earth-like civilization → alien civilization
```

Only through such a path can the project move from long-term speculation toward a researchable, verifiable, and reviewable engineering roadmap.
